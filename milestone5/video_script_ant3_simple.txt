Simple English Version

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[0:00-0:15] INTRODUCTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• We're solving a multi-robot coverage problem
• Three robots need to cover a 6 by 6 grid
• Some cells have obstacles - robots can't go there
• Goal: Cover every free cell efficiently

[0:15-0:30] WHAT IS ANT COLONY OPTIMIZATION?
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Inspired by real ants finding food
• Ants leave chemical trails called pheromone
• Other ants follow strong trails
• Strong trails get stronger over time

[0:30-0:50] HOW IT WORKS FOR ROBOTS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• We use "virtual ants" to explore solutions
• Each ant does two things:
  1. DARP: Divides cells among robots (who covers what)
  2. UF-STC: Builds paths for each robot (how to cover)

[0:50-1:10] DESIRABILITY FUNCTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Desirability (η) = 1 / (distance + γ × workload_imbalance)
• Combines two things:
  - How close cell is to robot start
  - How balanced the workload is
• Gamma (γ) controls balance vs distance preference
• Higher desirability = better choice

[1:10-1:30] PROBABILITY CALCULATION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Probability = (pheromone^α) × (desirability^β)
• Alpha (α): How much we trust past experience
• Beta (β): How much we trust desirability heuristic
• High α = follow strong pheromone trails
• High β = prefer closer, balanced assignments

[1:30-1:50] PHEROMONE UPDATE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Step 1: EVAPORATION
  - All pheromone decreases by factor ρ (rho)
  - Old information fades over time
  
• Step 2: DEPOSIT (Ant Quantity Model)
  - Each ant deposits pheromone = desirability value
  - Good cells get more pheromone
  - Future ants more likely to choose them

[1:50-2:10] THE ITERATION PROCESS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Run 50 iterations
• Each iteration:
  1. 15 ants explore solutions
  2. Calculate desirability for each cell-robot pair
  3. Use probability to assign cells
  4. Build paths with UF-STC
  5. Update pheromone (evaporate + deposit)
  6. Track best solution
• After 50 iterations: best solution found

[2:10-2:30] CASE STUDY 2 RESULTS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Ran algorithm 4 times to check reliability
• Results:
  ✓ 100% coverage - all 30 free cells covered
  ✓ Workload imbalance: 12.00 (robots share work fairly)
  ✓ Average runtime: 7.3 seconds (very fast!)
• Best solution:
  - Robot 0: covers 11 cells
  - Robot 1: covers 21 cells  
  - Robot 2: covers 19 cells
• All paths are valid - no obstacles hit, no jumps

[2:30-2:40] CONCLUSION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• In just 7 seconds, ACO found excellent solution
• Uses pheromone trails, desirability, and probability
• Covers everything efficiently
• Successfully balances coverage and workload
• All three robots work together perfectly
